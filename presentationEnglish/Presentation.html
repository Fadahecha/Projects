Acronyms

An acronym is a word or name formed from the initial components of a longer name or phrase. Acronyms are usually formed from the initial letters of words, but sometimes use syllables. They can also be a mixture, as in radar (RAdio Detection And Ranging), REGEX (Regular Expression). Acronyms can be pronounced as words, like NASA and UNESCO; as individual letters, like in GIF (GRAPHICS INTERCHANGE FORMAT), PNG (PORTABLE NETWORK GRAPHICS); and as both letters and words, like in JPEG (pronounced JAY-peg) . Also numbers count Numeronym: W3C (World Wide Web Consortium).

Although they serve the same purpose, an acronym is not the same as an abbreviation. The main point of reference is that acronyms form new words while abbreviations are merely a series of letters.
